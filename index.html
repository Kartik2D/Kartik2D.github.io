What happens when you cross kinaesthetics with interaction design?

Kinaesthetics is the study of the perception of one's own body motions. Interaction design is the study of how users and technology communicate with each other. Combine these two fields and you get something with a satisfying and wholesome flavor. I, and many others, know it as juicy design, but I like to think of it as UI/UX with UMAMI. So how exactly do I explore savory flavors in software? Take a look at my exploratory projects below!


A little bit about me first! I'm Kartik and I'm a generalist, which means I do a lot of things - a mile wide and an inch deep, if you will. My design philosphy is about using real world animation principles to help drive the flow of information between a human and a computer. It's about bringing principles of real-life charm into our digital world to provide fleeting moments of stimulus. The animation above is simply meant to showcase my animation timing skills. I paid special attention to offset the timing of every animated element.


Above: My experimental basketball game! This game is an exploration of how to program charming reactionary elements with primitive animation. For example: the bendy goalpost rope seen above is a simply a bezier curve with f=-kx applied to the midpoint.
